Yoga

How does Yoga improve our mental health?


A study by Dr. Richard Davidson of the University of Wisconsin found that yogis have increased activity in the prefrontal cortex. This finding is related to a higher level of well-being and better immune function. By improving the circulation of the endocrine glands, continuous yoga practice can enhance the function of hormones, which play a major role in the physiology of depression. This leads to a reduction in depression and an improvement in overall mood.




Step by step

  1. Sitting in sukhasana (easy pose), inhale, look up to the sky and lift the arms up;

  2. Drop the chin to the chest and breathe

  3. Begin to engage fully in pranayama, breathing ujjayi breath (victorious breath)

  4. Inhale into the heart; exhale and release the belly

  5. Take the hands behind your back and interlace the fingers opening up the shoulders

  6. Exhale into a forward bend

  7. Inhale sitting back upright, releasing the hands




Benefits of Yoga

What can we get from Yoga?


Up to now, it is no secret that regular yoga practice brings many physical benefits, such as improved flexibility, strength and posture. However, the psychological, cognitive, and emotional benefits of yoga are often overlooked. These may include:

  • Cognitive benefits, such as improved concentration and mental clarity, better memory and retention, and improved concentration

  • The decline of the self-destructive model

  • Greater creativity

  • Increase awareness and control of body and mind

  • Enhance the ability to exist in the moment

  • Increase emotional stability

  • Improve self-awareness and self-acceptance, including physical acceptance

  • Improve sleeping

  • View others more positively

  • Reduce depression

  • Reduce stress and anxiety, increase calm
