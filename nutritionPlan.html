Importance of Nutrition

Why we need a nutrition plan?

Our diet and mental health have a complicated relationship. However, there is a relationship between what we eat and how we feel, according to studies.

Our brain is affected by what we eat. Some meals can improve our mood. A Mediterranean-style diet that includes plenty of vegetables, seafood, fresh herbs, garlic, olive oil, cereals, and grains, as well as fish oil, can help to alleviate depressive symptoms.






What is Serotonin?

Seratonin is a neurotransmitter, or chemical messenger, that’s involved in many processes throughout your body, from regulating your mood to promoting smooth digestion.

It's also recognised for encouraging excellent sleep by assisting in the regulation of circadian rhythms, assisting in the regulation of hunger, contributing in the promotion of learning and memory, and helping in the promotion of pleasant moods and prosocial conduct.

Low Serotonin often make you feel:


  • Feeling jittery, down, or sad

  • Feeling angry or hostile

  • Sleep trouble or if you're tired

  • Feel irrational

  • A reduced appetite

  • Suffer from nausea and stomach problems



Strategies to boost serotonin levels

  1. Food

    You can't acquire serotonin directly from food, but you can receive tryptophan, an amino acid that your brain converts to serotonin. High-protein meals, such as turkey and salmon, are high in tryptophan.

    However, due to the blood-brain barrier, it's not as easy as consuming tryptophan-rich meals. This is a protective sheath that surrounds your brain and regulates what enters and exits it.

    In a nutshell, foods high in tryptophan are generally also high in other amino acids. These other amino acids are more likely than tryptophan to penetrate the blood-brain barrier because they are more plentiful.

    However, there may be a method to circumvent the mechanism. According to research, combining carbohydrates with meals high in tryptophan may enable more tryptophan to reach your brain. Consume 25 to 30 grammes of carbs with tryptophan-rich foods.

  2. Exercise

    Exercising causes tryptophan to be released into your bloodstream. It may also reduce the quantity of other amino acids in the body. More tryptophan will be able to enter your brain as a result of this.

    Aerobic activity at a level you're comfortable with appears to have the biggest impact, so dust off those roller skates or sign up for a dancing class. The objective is to raise your heart rate.
