Meditation

A way of self-regulating the body and mind

Mindfulness meditation is a way of self-regulating the body and mind. As a clinical intervention, mindfulness meditation has been shown to have a beneficial effect on the mental and physical state, especially in terms of mood improvement and recovery from emotion-related psychopathology. Mindfulness meditation has been shown to promote happiness and emotional balance, reduce stress responsiveness, and reduce negative emotions related to anxiety and depression.





Benefits of Meditation

What can we get from meditation?



























  1. Reduce stress

    2. Decompression is one of the most common reasons people try to meditate. Normally, mental and physical stress leads to increased levels of the stress hormone cortisol. This can produce many harmful effects of stress, such as the release of inflammatory chemicals called cytokines. These effects can disrupt sleep, promote depression and anxiety, increase blood pressure, and cause fatigue and blurred thinking. In addition, research shows that meditation can also improve the symptoms of stress-related diseases, including irritable bowel syndrome, post-traumatic stress disorder, and fibromyalgia.

  2. Control anxiety

    3. Meditation can reduce stress levels, thereby reducing anxiety. An analysis of nearly 1,300 adults found that meditation may reduce anxiety. In addition, 8 weeks of mindfulness meditation helped to reduce the anxiety symptoms of patients with generalized anxiety disorder, while increasing positive self-report and improving stress response and coping ability. Employees who used the mindfulness meditation app for 8 weeks improved their well-being and stress. And work pressure is reduced. Another study of 47 chronic pain patients found that completing an 8-week meditation program can significantly improve depression, anxiety, and pain for more than 1 year.

  3. Promote emotional health

    4. Certain forms of meditation can improve self-image and a more positive outlook on life. For example, a review of the treatment of more than 3,500 adults found that mindfulness meditation improved symptoms of depression. Similarly, people who received meditation therapy experienced a reduction in depressive symptoms. Another study found that compared with the control group, those who completed the meditation practice had fewer negative thoughts when viewing negative images. In addition, inflammatory chemicals called cytokines released under stress can affect mood and cause depression. A review of several studies shows that meditation can also reduce depression by reducing the levels of these inflammatory chemicals.
