Mental Health

Mental health of international student

International students encounter a variety of mental illness in Australia.

This videos highlight some of the symptoms and behaviours people may experience due to changes to their situation, stress or isolation, and identify tips and resources available that can provide much needed support.




Stress level testing

Want to measure your stress levels?

You may like to measure your current stress levels. The Stress Symptoms Checklist lists 52 physical and mental symptoms of stress. Check all the symptoms you have felt over the past month and then count up your total score. You can then check your stress levels using the table at the bottom.

Solutions

How to reduce the stress and improve your mental health?

When studying abroad, it's natural to feel worried or homesick from time to time. When dealing with a completely different lifestyle, academic expectations, financial challenges, the stress of juggling job and study responsibilities, and relationship concerns, it is normal for foreign students to feel nervous. It's alright if you feel like you need a little more support now and again. According to Forbes-Mewett & Sawyer's research, foreign students are more likely to experience stress as a result of the additional obstacles they confront when living abroad.


International students encounter a variety of pressures. Here are some of the ways to handle them.

  1. 1. Maintain a social network in your host nation.

    Build a local support system or share tales with other foreign students who have gone through similar situations. You'll also be able to meet students from your country and other surrounding countries who are studying in and around your city. Find foreign student groups or people in your city who share similar interests via social networking sites/apps.

  2. 2. Seek assistance from friends, family, and/or personal networks.

    Speak with a close friend or family member. And try activities like –

    • • Weekly/monthly scheduling Sessions with family or friends through video calling applications.

    • • Traveling and mailing letters back to family and friends.

    • • Composing emails and letters.

    • • For a while, Try turning off your social media.

    • • Getting involved with student groups.

    • • Making new friends.

    • • With new friends, travel and discover.

    • • Be willing to try new things.

  3. 3. Exercise on a regular basis, eat well, and take care of yourself.

    Stress is also known to be triggered by poor eating habits and sleep deprivation. So, eat well-balanced meals and get enough of rest. Instead of dining out frequently or bingeing on takeout, try making your meals at home. Physical and mental health both benefit from exercise. Maintain your fitness by going for a walk/run, swimming, or working out at the gym. Meditation, yoga, and deep breathing techniques might also assist you in being calm and collected.

  4. 4. Become acquainted with your new surroundings.

    Make a concentrated effort to become acquainted with your new surroundings and the current events in your host city. It will make you feel more connected and less like an outsider if you familiarise yourself. Find out what your new neighbourhood has to offer if you were a member of a club/church/group back home. Research your current area and choose a few sites you should visit, such as the finest coffee shops in the city, renowned hangout spots for local street artists, or all the varied hiking opportunities. Make a list of these locations or activities and set a goal for yourself to see/do them all before leaving.

  5. 5. Share how you're feeling with others.

    Build a local support system or share tales with other foreign students who have gone through similar situations. You'll also be able to meet students from your country and other surrounding countries who are studying in and around your city. Find foreign student groups or people in your city who share similar interests via social networking sites/apps.

  6. 6. Allow yourself to vent and express your emotions.

    If you're too timid to share your feelings with others, keep a diary and write everything down. To express yourself, you can write poetry, tales, or even engage in creative art forms such as painting.
